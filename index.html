Chapter Two: The Anchor

my handwriting

before you valentines was just a date on the calendar ...

“with you, it wasn’t even about the 14th. It was about me waking up every day knowing my heart belonged to someone, someone like you. For the first time, it didn’t feel like a loss of independence, but a gain of gravity as if loving you, anchored me instead of weaken me. Because it was never about roses or the 14th or whatever the world decided love should look like it was about the certainty of you. The way ordinary mornings felt claimed the way my thoughts stopped to focus on you.”

the continuation

It’s strange, isn’t it? How the world tells us that love is supposed to be this grand, sweeping gesture—a fireworks display, a dramatic declaration. And while I wouldn't say no to fireworks, I’ve learned that the most profound proof of us isn't in the spectacle. It’s in the silence. It’s in the way you reach for my hand in the car without looking. It’s in the sound of your laugh from the other room when you’re watching something stupid on your phone, a sound that immediately makes me smile, even if I have no idea what’s funny.

I’ve been thinking about that word: gravity. I used it because it’s the only one that fits. Before you, I felt like I was floating—not in a bad way, just… untethered. I was a collection of habits and routines, a person moving through the world. And then you arrived, and suddenly, I wasn’t floating anymore. I was in orbit. You gave me a center to circle around, not to lose myself, but to find a new kind of motion. A purposeful one.

This second letter, this second chapter, feels different from the first, doesn’t it? The first one was about the spark, the discovery, the breathtaking rush of falling. This one… this one is about the landing. It’s about the day after the fall, when you look around and realize the ground you’ve landed on is softer than any cloud you were ever floating on. It’s about the quiet, confident knowledge that has settled into my bones: He is here. He is mine. And I am his.

Thank you for being my gravity. Thank you for the quiet mornings and the loud laughter. Thank you for making the ordinary feel sacred. I can’t wait to see what the next chapter brings.

— All my anchored heart, yours.
