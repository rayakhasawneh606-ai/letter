✧ chapter two: gravity ✧
from my journal, handwritten

“before you, valentines was just a date on the calendar that couples like to celebrate but with you, it wasn’t even about the 14th. It was about me waking up every day knowing my heart belonged to someone, someone like you. For the first time, it didn’t feel like a loss of independence, but a gain of gravity as if loving you, anchored me instead of weaken me. Because it was never about roses or the 14th or whatever the world decided love should look like it was about the certainty of you. The way ordinary mornings felt claimed the way my thoughts stopped to focus on you.”

my love, Ahmer

It’s strange, isn’t it? How the world tells us that love is supposed to be a fireworks display, a dramatic declaration. But I’ve learned that the most profound proof of us isn’t in the spectacle. It’s in the silence. It’s the way you reach for my hand in the car without looking. It’s the sound of your laugh from the other room, a sound that immediately makes me smile.

Gravity — that’s what you are. Before you, I was floating, untethered. Then you arrived and gave me a center to circle around. Not to lose myself, but to find a new kind of motion. A purposeful one.

This second letter is about the landing. The quiet confidence that has settled in my bones: He is here. He is mine. And I am his.

Thank you for the quiet mornings, the loud laughter, and for making the ordinary feel sacred. I can’t wait to see what the next chapters bring.

— forever anchored, Raya
